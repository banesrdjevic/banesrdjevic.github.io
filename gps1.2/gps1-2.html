What is pair programming?

Pair programming is two or more people coming together to work on a project. This enables faster proofing as well as a faster way to join ideas. Plus, one person might be strong where another is weak and vice versa, in which case they would learn from each other.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programing is important to DBC because it offers a chance to practice collaborative programming in a real-world setting. Plus, DBC encourages learning from each other as much as learning from the staff.

Compare Driver and Navigator

The navigator holds the idea for the programming session in their head and describes what they want to the driver. This allows the driver to focus on the code and not worry about strategy, and vice versa.
